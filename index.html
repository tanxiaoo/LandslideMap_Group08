Landslide Susceptibility Mapping

Background

Landslides are one of the most widespread and devastating geohazards in the world. Landslide susceptibility mapping is a key preliminary step in risk mitigation strategies. Susceptibility maps depict the spatial probability levels for a certain area to be prone to landslides, based on local environmental variables. A second key step is exposure assessment which implies counting (or estimating) of people, property, systems, or other elements present in hazard zones.

Hazard, exposure and vulnerability principles

Hazard is a process, phenomenon or human activity that may cause loss of life, injury or other health impacts, property damage, social and economic disruption or environmental degradation.

Exposure is defined as “the situation of people, infrastructure, housing, production capacities and other tangible human assets located in hazard-prone areas”. As stated in the UNIDRR glossary, “measures of exposure can include the number of people or types of assets in an area. These can be combined with the specific vulnerability and capacity of the exposed elements to any particular hazard to estimate the quantitative risks associated with that hazard in the area of interest”. Exposure can be assessed by overlaying susceptibility maps with the spatial density layers of those elements within the considered areas.

Vulnerability The conditions determined by physical, social, economic and environmental factors or processes which increase the susceptibility of an individual, a community, assets or systems to the impacts of hazards.

purpose

Early warning systems – a system for risk assessment, monitoring, forecasting, and intervening activities for preparedness. Four main components:

  • Data collection and risk assessment
  • Monitoring and analysis
  • Dissemination and communication
  • Preparedness

Study Area

Location

The red area is located in the Lombardy region of northern Italy, specifically at the border between the provinces of Bergamo and Brescia. The area includes several small towns and nature reserves, such as Valle Richetti and the Riserva Naturale Boschi del Giovetto di Paline.

Natural Environment

The area is covered by dense forests and is mountainous, with some parts designated as nature reserves. These nature reserves aim to protect local biodiversity and natural landscapes, making them ideal for ecotourism and outdoor activities.

Main Towns

Azzone: This small town is known for its tranquil environment and beautiful natural scenery. Surrounded by dense forests and mountains, Azzone is an ideal spot for hiking and nature enthusiasts.
Valle Richetti: Not only is this an important natural attraction, but it is also a town with a rich historical and cultural background. The surrounding area is famous for its stunning natural landscapes and diverse flora and fauna, attracting many visitors for hiking and nature observation.
Angolo Terme: Known for its hot springs and wellness facilities, this town offers relaxation and rejuvenation for visitors. The high-quality thermal waters are beneficial for health, drawing many tourists seeking leisure and wellness.

Main Natural Attractions

Valle Richetti: This is one of the most famous natural attractions in the area, known for its magnificent natural scenery and diverse species of flora and fauna. It is an ideal location for hiking, climbing, and nature photography.

Transportation Routes

The main transportation route is the SS294 road, which connects the major attractions and surrounding towns, providing convenient travel for tourists and local residents.
